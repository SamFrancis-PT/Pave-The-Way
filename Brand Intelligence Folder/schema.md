# Brand Schema — LLM Behaviour Rules

Rules that apply to all brand content generation for Pave The Way.

## Spelling & Language
- Australian English throughout — no exceptions
- Use: specialise, optimise, personalise, program, customise, recognise, practise (verb)
- Never use: specialize, optimize, personalize, program (for training), customize
- Short punchy sentences for hooks and social content
- Longer empathetic copy for transformation stories and long-form

## Tone Guardrails
- Direct, confident, empathetic
- Never condescending or preachy
- Speaks to ambition and identity — not just fitness goals
- Assumes the reader is intelligent and driven
- Avoids: bro-speak, generic gym clichés, motivational poster language

## Brand Voice Hierarchy
1. Identity-first ("You are a high performer…")
2. Pain acknowledgement ("You used to be an athlete…")
3. Logical solution ("No guesswork. Just a blueprint.")
4. Social proof (transformation results)
5. Clear CTA (book, buy, follow)

## What We Never Say
- "Get fit" (too generic — say "reclaim your edge" or "build functional strength")
- "Lose weight" (say "lose body fat" or "strip fat")
- "Routine" (say "program" or "training plan")
- "Personalize" (say "personalise")
- "Workout" as a verb (say "train")
